    Isaac Gracie Facts

  • He was raised alongside his brother and sister in Ealing, West London by his mother Judith, who was a poet and also worked as a psychotherapist.
  • His father left him and his family when he was quite young. Later, his stepfather also betrayed his mother.
  • Isaac embraced his mother’s passion for writing poems and continued to dwell on it. He also joined the University of East Anglia in order to study creative writing and it wasn’t long before he started to use his blessed ability to write songs.
  • He was 7 years old when he started singing in a choir and by the age of 16, he was leading choir. However, he was forced to leave after he lost his incredibly high note voice one fine Sunday. Later, he wanted to continue after the age of 18, as a tenor but found the guitar and never went back ever since.
  • Isaac is a self-taught guitarist who learned his chords while listening to songs by Jeff Buckley, Radiohead, and Bob Dylan.
  • He was discovered through Soundcloud, after his self-penned song Last Words went viral on it.
  • He once slammed his guitar on the floor and walked out during a high school music contest not knowing why the judge had interrupted him in between. Later, he found out that the reason why the judge stopped him was that he wanted to tell Isaac that he was going to win the competition and that was exactly what happened.
  • Prior to becoming a full-time music artist, he worked as a barista in a coffee shop.
  • Most of his debut self-titled album was recorded at Rak Studios in St. Johns Wood.
  • He derives his inspiration from love.
  • Isaac is fond of listening to music from the Arctic Monkeys, Nick Cave, MGMT, and Pearl Jam.
  • Despite being a non-religious kind of person, he always wears a crucifix around his neck.
  • In 2016, he signed a record deal with Virgin EMI Records.
