Early Life, Family, and Education

Born on February 17, 1995, Sony Michel is a renowned American former football player known for his exceptional skills in the running back position.

Hailing from a close-knit family, Sony was born to Jean Michel and Marie Michel and spent his formative years in Florida.

He shares a special bond with his half-sister Lamise and brother Marken, who also pursued a career in professional football.

Sony’s family roots trace back to Haiti, as his parents were Haitian immigrants. They met in 1991, and their son Marken was born in 1995.

Seeking a better life and brighter future for their children, Jean and Marie made the courageous decision to immigrate to the U.S.

As Sony’s passion for football developed, he enrolled at American Heritage High School in Plantation, Florida, where he honed his athletic abilities.

In addition to football, he displayed his versatility by excelling in various track and field events during his high school years.

After an impressive journey in high school, Sony’s talents caught the attention of the University of Georgia, where he continued to shine on the football field, making a significant impact in college football.

Collegiate & Professional Career

Sony Michel began his football career early, excelling in school and earning five-star recruit status.

At the University of Georgia, he showed his talent, overcoming an injury to record impressive stats.

In October 2017, he had a breakout year, named Rose Bowl Offensive MVP for his remarkable performance.

Michel’s journey from a young enthusiast to a collegiate standout showcases his dedication and potential in football.

Professional Career & Injury

In the 2018 NFL Draft, Sony was selected as the 31st overall pick by the New England Patriots.

Despite missing the entire preseason and season-opener due to a knee injury, he made a strong debut year, amassing six touchdowns and 931 rushing yards, along with seven receptions for 50 yards.

The following season, Michel continued to impress, achieving 912 rushing yards, seven touchdowns, and 12 receptions for 94 yards.

Notably, he led the Patriots to victory with notable performances against teams like the Miami Dolphins and Washington Redskins.

However, injuries have been a part of Michel’s football journey.

He suffered an ACL injury during high school and faced a fractured shoulder blade in 2014, causing him to miss several games at the University of Georgia.

Despite these setbacks, Michel’s determination and resilience remained unwavering.

In 2020, expectations were high for Michel to play a prominent role in the Patriots offense after the departure of Tom Brady.

However, due to off-season foot surgery, he began the season on the PUP list, delaying his return to the field.

In a significant move, Michel was traded to the Los Angeles Rams in August 2021, where he now dons the number 25 jersey.

Throughout his football career, Michel’s dedication and self-motivation have been evident, constantly pushing himself to improve and overcome challenges.

Highlights, Awards, and Achievements

Throughout his football career, Sony Michel has received numerous honors and awards.

In 2013, he was named first-team All-USA and Colorado Gatorade Player of the Year, as well as a semifinalist for the 2014 U.S. Army Player of the Year.

He earned SEC Freshman of the Week in 2014 and won the SEC Championship in 2017. In 2018, he clinched the Rose Bowl Championship and was named the Rose Bowl Game MVP.

Michel’s most remarkable achievement came as a rookie, breaking the NFL record for the most playoff rushing touchdowns.

Notably, he scored five touchdowns in the conference and divisional championship games.

He capped it off with a touchdown in Super Bowl LIII, leading his team to victory on February 3, 2019.

Retirement

After an eventful five seasons on the gridiron, former New England Patriots running back Sony Michel has decided to retire from the NFL.

The announcement was made by Los Angeles Rams head coach Sean McVay on a Saturday.

Since being drafted as the Patriots’ first-round selection (no. 31 overall) in the 2018 NFL Draft, Michel had his fair share of highs and lows.

Notably, he played a crucial role in New England’s triumphant run to victory in Super Bowl LIII, even setting an NFL record for rushing touchdowns in the postseason with an impressive six.

However, injuries and inconsistency occasionally marred his time with the Patriots.

Despite the challenges, Michel proved to be a productive running back for New England, amassing 2,689 rushing yards on 620 carries and scoring 20 touchdowns over three seasons.

Nonetheless, the 2021 season marked a turning point as the team declined Michel’s fifth-year contract option, signaling his impending departure.

Subsequently, Michel was traded to the Los Angeles Rams in August in exchange for draft picks in the 2022 Draft.

During the 2021 season with the Rams, he managed 845 rushing yards and four rushing touchdowns, along with 128 receiving yards and one receiving touchdown.

In May 2022, Michel briefly signed with the Miami Dolphins, only to join the Los Angeles Chargers later that August.

His journey led him back to the Rams in June 2023 for a second stint, but ultimately, he has chosen to retire at the age of 28.

As he hangs up his cleats, fans and teammates alike will remember his significant contributions to the game throughout his career.
