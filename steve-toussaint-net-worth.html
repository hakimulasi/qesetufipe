So, how much is Steve Toussaint worth?

British actor Steve Toussaint has earned most of his money thanks to various high-profile roles in TV and film.

He started his career in the 1990s, playing Steve Dixie in the 1994 miniseries the Memoirs of Sherlock Holmes.

Most of his roles in the nineties were small credits, but he landed his first major TV gig in 1994 when he landed the role of Barry Christie in The Knock.

Toussaint featured in 37 episodes between 1994 and 2000. The show was popular at the turn of the century in the UK, following the trials and tribulations of HMRC officers who were trying to prevent criminals from smuggling drugs into the country.

Toussaint also played the role of Joe in five episodes of the 1999 British TV series Jack of Hearts, cementing his place as a popular TV actor in the UK at the time. 

Several more roles in TV shows and films followed in the early 2000s for Toussaint, including credits in Circus, Dog Eat Dog, and The Bill.

Although most of his roles have been in British TV and film, he played Judge Hugo Kemp in three episodes of CSI: Miami in 2007 and 2008, before returning to UK screens in the likes of Holby City, New Tricks, and Skins.

During the 2010s, Toussaint continued his impressive acting career, playing Richard Parkwood in Silent Witness, Mallick in Line of Duty, and DSI Will Pemberton in Scott & Bailey.

In 2015, he landed another role in a cop show, featuring as CS Moody in six episodes of Lewis. 

This has been a recurring theme throughout Toussaint’s career, and he has often played roles on the right side of the law since his debut in The Knock in the nineties.

This is due to the fact that he portrays roles in office with a great deal of charisma and authority, which has seen him establish himself as a renowned actor in many British legal dramas as a result. 

Another unique aspect of Toussaint’s career is that he has lent his voice to several video games. He played the voice of George Lambert in Jurassic World Evolution and also featured in the World of Warcraft: Battle for Azeroth.
