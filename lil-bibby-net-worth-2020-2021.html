Bibby was born in Chicago on July 18, 1994. He had a difficult childhood, bearing witness to drug abuse. He is currently pursuing engineering at Illinois State University.

Career

The title of Bibby’s first mixed tape, “Free Crack,” was inspired by Kanye West’s song track “Crack Music.” Incidentally, both of them hail from Chicago. The artists Lil Herb and King L both made guest appearances in Bibby’s first tape. The production of this was handled by the likes of Young Chop, The Olympics, Hit-Boy, among others. It was even hosted by DJ Scream.

In the year 2014, he collaborated with rapper Juicy J and made an appearance in the remix of “Main Chick,” which was performed by Kid Ink. The year also saw the release of his second mixtape, “Free Crack 2”. This tape saw guest appearances from Lil Herb, Anthony Hamilton, Wiz Khalifa, Kevin Gates, Juicy J, and even Jadakiss, among others. It was produced by the likes of Jadakiss himself, Honorable C.N.O.T.E., Sony Digital, and others.

He released “Free Crack 3” in November 2015. In the same year, he also released two singles, “Ridah” and “Aww Man.”

These mixtapes led the path to a record deal with RCA label Kemosabe Records, which was responsible for releasing his first studio album, “FC3: The Epilogue”, in 2015.

Awards & Achievements

After the release of his debut tape, Lil Bibby was on the front cover of “Vibe” magazine, which showered accolades on him, stating he is one of the rappers to watch in 2014. He was also hailed as having the chops for being a major music artist.

It was also in 2014 that reports revealed that Drake himself was a fan of Bibby’s work and that he had spoken to Bibby before his tape’s release, which gave Bibby the confidence boost that he needed.

In the same year, he appeared on Sway’s Morning show, “Sway.” He was interviewed by Billboard and appeared in XXL’s 2014 Freshman Class. The magazine awarded his second mixed tape an XL, admiring its new outlook and originality.
