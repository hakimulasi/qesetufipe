Biography:

Michaela Conlin was born on June 9, 1978 in Allentown, Pennsylvania, United States. She made her first acting experience on a stage play when she was 6. Since then she has performed on stage in community and regional products in Pennsylvania. She received her training from New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts. In 2001, she made her screen debut in the movie Love the Hard Way. She also made her first TV appearance in an episode of Law & Order.

Her first main role on the small screen was as Dr. Maggie Yang on the short-lived medical drama series MDs. Subsequently, she had a recurring role on The D.A.. She got her break in 2005 after landing the role of Angela Montenegro on the crime-drama Bones. She still plays the role up to present. As a film actress, she had appeared in Enchanted and The Lincoln Lawyer and a few low-budget films.
