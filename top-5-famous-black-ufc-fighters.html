5: Francis Ngannou

Francis Ngannou, also known as “The Predator,” is a Cameroonian-French MMA who contends in the heavyweight regiment. He was born September 5, 1986, in Cameroon.

Standing 6 feet 4 inches tall and weighing around 260 pounds, Ngannou possesses extraordinary punching power and has finished many of his opponents in the first round.

His impressive combat technique and humble demeanor outside the octagon have earned him a fan choice in the UFC.

He is another name who is ranking high among the famous black UFC fighters who have made pathways.

Ngannou has faced some of the toughest heavyweights in the world, including Stipe Miocic, Cain Velasquez, Junior dos Santos, and Alistair Overeem.

As of my last update, Ngannou’s professional MMA record is approximately 17 wins and three losses.

Ngannou’s journey to the top of the UFC heavyweight division is a testament to his incredible talent and dedication to the sport.

There is no doubt about his achievement and he remains part of the famous black UFC fighters who still has a career going on.

4: Usman Kamaru

Kamaru Usman, known as “The Nigerian Nightmare,” is a fighter competing in the welterweight section of the UFC.

He was born May 11, 1987, in Nigeria. Usman relocated to the United States to pursue his MMA career.

He pursued a career which today many know him as part of the famous black UFC fighters who paved the way.

Usman started his professional MMA career in 2012 and quickly gained attention for his wrestling crafts and insane combatting technique.

He is known for his suffocating ground control and powerful striking, making him one of the most formidable competitors in the welterweight division.

Usman has faced and defeated top-ranked welterweights such as Tyron Woodley, Jorge Masvidal, Colby Covington, and Gilbert Burns throughout his career.

As of my last update, Usman boasts an impressive professional MMA record with around 20 wins and one loss.

His combination of elite grappling, striking, and cardio has cemented his status as one of the best pound-for-pound fighters in the world.

Usman’s journey from Nigeria to becoming a dominant UFC champion is an inspiring story of perseverance and dedication to his craft.

He has made a place for himself as a pioneer when it comes to famous black UFC fighters.

3: Israel Adesanya

Israel Adesanya called “The Last Stylebender,” is a Nigeria-born MMA and former kickboxer.

He was born on July 22, 1989, in Nigeria before relocating to New Zealand at a young age with his family.

His ability to be able to make his career successful places him on the list of famous black UFC fighters.

Throughout his UFC career, Adesanya has faced tough opponents such as Yoel Romero, Paulo Costa, and Marvin Vettori, showcasing his versatility and adaptability inside the octagon.

As of my last update, Adesanya holds an impressive professional MMA record with around 21 wins and two losses.

His charisma, skill, and unique fighting style have made him a fan favorite and one of the top pound-for-pound fighters in the world.

Adesanya’s journey from kickboxing prodigy to UFC champion is a testament to his dedication and talent in combat sports.

His unique style makes him another of the famous black UFC fighters who have come in to win Championships.

2: Demetrious Johnson

Demetrious Johnson is an MMA fighter who has competed in various promotions, notably the UFC.

Johnson was born August 13, 1986, in Kentucky and he began his MMA career fighting in 2007.

Johnson initially competed in the bantamweight and flyweight divisions before establishing himself as one of the greatest flyweight fighters in MMA history.

He established himself among the best famous black UFC fighters. During his time in the UFC, Johnson became the inaugural flyweight Champion in September 2012 by winning the Flyweight Tournament.

1: Jon Jones

Jonathan Dwight Jones is a mixed martial fighter widely considered one of the most extraordinary warriors on record.

Jones was born July 19, 1987, in New York and his journey kicked off professionally in 2008.

Jones gained prominence in the UFC light heavyweight division, quickly rising through the ranks due to his exceptional athleticism, unorthodox fighting style, and versatile skill set.

He went on to defend the title multiple times and became known for his dominance and creativity inside the octagon.

Jones has defeated a who’s who of the light heavyweight division, including Daniel Cormier, Alexander Gustafsson, and Quinton “Rampage” Jackson.

He is also known for his rivalry with Cormier, with whom he had two highly anticipated fights.

As of my last update, Jon Jones holds a professional MMA record of around 26 wins, one loss, and one no-contest. His success placed him as part of the famous black UFC fighters.
