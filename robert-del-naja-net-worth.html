Robert Del Naja Finance Overview

Massive Attack’s Commercial Success

Massive Attack is central to Robert Del Naja’s financial success. The band, formed in the late 1980s, became pioneers of the trip-hop genre. Their debut album, Blue Lines (1991), received critical acclaim and laid the groundwork for their future success.

The album Mezzanine (1998) is particularly notable for its commercial success, selling nearly 4 million copies. These album sales, along with global tours and live performances, have significantly contributed to Del Naja’s wealth.

Contributions to Film Scores and Soundtracks

Del Naja has also earned considerable income from his work on film scores. His collaborations with Neil Davidge and Euan Dickinson have produced acclaimed soundtracks for films like Gomorrah and Danny the Dog.

The song Herculaneum for the film Gomorrah won the David Di Donatello award for best song, highlighting his skill and earning potential in this area.

Artistic Ventures and Speculations

Before his music career, Del Naja was a pioneering graffiti artist. His art has been featured in various exhibitions and has contributed to his reputation and income.
