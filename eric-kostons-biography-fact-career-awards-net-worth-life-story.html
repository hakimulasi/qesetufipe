Thai American professional skateboarder, Eric Koston has an estimated net worth of $15 million.

He began skating in 1986 and remains active. He was first sponsored in 1991 and turned pro in 1993. He has appeared in multiple video games, television shows, and videos, including “Tony Hawk” video game series and “EA” Skate 2 and Skate 3. Koston co-owns Fourstar Clothing and also co-owns the skatepark “The Philo-Thai Centre” with professional skater Gavin Davies. He has won a number of championships, including 1st place at the 2000 X Games, the 2000 & 2003 Gravity Games, the 2002 Gravity Games Street, and the 2003 X Games Street Competition. In addition, he is sponsor by several companies including, Girl Skateboards, Independent Trucks, Spitfire Wheels, Nike SB Shoes, Oakley sunglasses, Skullcandy Headphones and Jessup Griptape. He has designed many pro model shoes such as the Lakai Koston Select and the éS Koston. He is considered by many the Billy Ruff of street skateboarding.
