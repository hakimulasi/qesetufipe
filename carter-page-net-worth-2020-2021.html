Early Life & Biography

Born as Carter William Page to Allan Robert Page and Rachel Page on 3rd June 1971 in Minneapolis, Minnesota, he was raised in Poughkeepsie, New York where his father worked as a manager and executive of Central Hudson Gas and Electric Company. He completed his schooling from “Our Lady of Lourdes High School” in 1989 after which he proceeded to complete his graduation in Bachelor of Science from the United States Naval Academy in 1993.

Here, he topped among the top 10% in his class and was therefore chosen for the Navy’s Trident Scholar program, the speciality of the program is it gives very selected officers an opportunity to pursue independent academic study and research. After being a senior in Naval Academy, he worked in the office of a former US Secretary of Defence Les Aspin Jr as a researcher for a department responsible for funding and oversight of the Department of Defence called as the House Armed Services Committee.

He served in the US Navy for five years. It was during his work as an intelligent officer for a United States Peacekeeping operation in Morocco that rewarded him the rank of lieutenant. In 1994 he completed his M.A at Georgetown University in Washington DC in the field of National Security Studies. In 1988, he left active duty in the navy and became a member of the Navy’s inactive reserve till the year 2004.
