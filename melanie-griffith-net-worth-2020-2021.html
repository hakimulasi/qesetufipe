Early life

Melanie Griffith was born on August 9, 1959, in Manhattan to actress Tippi Hedren and stage actor and advertisement executive Peter Griffith. After her parents divorced when she was only four years old, she alternatively lived with her father in New York and sometimes in Antelope Valley California with her mother.

Melanie Griffith worked as a child model but left due to extreme shyness. Later she joined Hollywood professional school where she did well and was a graduate at the young age of 16.

Career

Although she acted in some films starting in the year 1969 yet a significant role came to her only in 1975 in the film ‘night moves’ in which she did some racy nude scenes. This was a beginning to her some other films in which she was typecast as a nymphet, like in the movie ‘downing pool’ (1975) and ‘One on one’ (1977).

During the shooting of the film Roar (1981) she was attacked by a lion resulting in 50 stitches on her face. Her career was adversely affected by her drug and alcohol addiction, but in 1984 she made a comeback in the film ‘Body double’ for which National Society of film critics awarded her best-supporting actress Award.

In 1986 she starred in the film ‘something wild’ which became a hit movie winning her wide acclaim. More success came to her with the film ‘working girl’ in 1988 for which she won Golden Globe award – best actress for her role and was also nominated for Academy award. She continued to work in mainstream films and her TV movie ‘Buffalo Girls’ (1995) received a Golden Globe nomination.

Her role in the film ‘Another day in paradise’ (1998) in which she acted as a free-spirited heroin user is regarded as one of the best in her career. In 2003 she made her Broadway debut with the musical ‘Chicago’ in the role of Roxie which received wide critical acclaim.

In 2017 she co-starred with Al Pacino in the film ‘Pirates of Somalia.’
